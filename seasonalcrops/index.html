Seasonal Crops in India

Learn about the best crops to grow in each season to maximize your yield.

Spring Season Crops (Feb to April)

  • Wheat - Ideal for the cool weather at the beginning of the season.
  • Barley - Grows well in temperate climates, ideal for spring.
  • Cucumber - Best suited for the moderate temperatures in spring.
  • Mustard - Thrives in the cool to moderate weather of spring.
  • Sunflower - Flourishes in the early warmth of spring.

Summer Season Crops (April to June)

  • Paddy (Rice) - Requires abundant sunlight and warm temperatures.
  • Millet (Bajra) - Heat-tolerant and thrives in the high temperatures.
  • Maize - Grows well in the hot and dry climate of Indian summers.
  • Pulses (Moong Dal) - Short-duration crop that benefits from summer warmth.
  • Groundnut - Thrives in the heat and requires good sunlight exposure.

Monsoon Season Crops (June to September)

  • Rice - Needs a large amount of water, ideal for monsoon rains.
  • Soybean - Benefits from the high moisture levels during monsoons.
  • Jute - Requires abundant water and thrives in humid climates.
  • Sugarcane - Grows well in wet and warm monsoon conditions.
  • Cotton - Requires warm weather and benefits from the rainy season.

Winter Season Crops (October to February)

  • Carrot - Prefers the cooler winter temperatures for proper growth.
  • Peas - Grows best in the cool and crisp weather of winter.
  • Garlic - Thrives in cool climates and grows well during the winter months.
  • Onion - Prefers the cooler weather and reduced sunlight of winter.
  • Spinach - Grows quickly in cool temperatures and is ideal for winter.
